The Problem

Why does this agent exist?

CVS receives thousands of Purchase Orders from vendors every week. When a vendor fails to deliver 100% of what was ordered or delivers late, an exception is generated that can cause out-of-stock at stores, lost sales, and friction with the buying team.

  • The supply chain team cannot manually review every PO exception
  • Rules exist but don't generate explanations — just flags
  • The buyer needs to know exactly what action to take and why
  • Today the decision is manual, subjective, and slow
85%
Fill Rate Threshold
If the vendor delivers less than 85% of ordered quantity, it's an exception
+5 days
Delay Threshold
If the PO arrives more than 5 days after the requested date, it's an exception

Classification Logic

Business rules to implement

Late Only
RECEIPT_DELAY_DAYS > 5 AND FILL_RATE >= 85
Suggested action: Vendor Outreach — contact vendor to track shipment and prevent recurrence
Low Fill Rate
FILL_RATE < 85 AND RECEIPT_DELAY_DAYS <= 5
Suggested action: Vendor Outreach or Escalate to Merchant depending on DNP_RANKING and OOS_LIKELY
Both (Critical)
FILL_RATE < 85 AND RECEIPT_DELAY_DAYS > 5
Suggested action: Escalate to Merchant — high OOS risk, requires buyer intervention
Clean
FILL_RATE >= 85 AND RECEIPT_DELAY_DAYS <= 5
No active exception — can be used as a negative case in evaluation

Escalation Factors

  • DNP Top 10% — High-margin SKU: always escalate if OOS risk is present
  • OOS_LIKELY = Y — Imminent stockout: top priority regardless of fill rate
  • AVG_FILL_RATE_8WK < 80 — Recurring issue: the LLM should explicitly mention it
  • STORE_COUNT > 100 — Many stores impacted: increases recommendation urgency
  • NEW_ISSUE_FLAG — New vs recurring issue changes the tone of the recommendation
Implementation tip: Build the rules in pure Python first (no LLM) and verify that your classification matches COMBINED_ACTION in at least 75% of cases before integrating the LLM.
06
Prompt Engineering

How to structure the LLM prompt

Reference Template
### System You are a supply chain analyst specialized in vendor compliance. Your goal is to explain Purchase Order exceptions clearly and recommend the correct action. ### User Analyze this PO exception: Vendor: {VENDOR_NAME} PO: {PO_NBR} | SKU: {SKU_NBR} | DC: {DC_ID} Category: {CATEGORY} | Priority: {DNP_RANKING} Current Fill Rate: {FILL_RATE}% 4-Week Average: {AVG_FILL_RATE_4WK}% Delay: {RECEIPT_DELAY_DAYS} days OOS Risk: {OOS_LIKELY} Total WOS: {TOTAL_WEEKS_OF_SUPPLY} weeks Root Cause: {COMBINED_CAUSE} Generate: (1) a 2-3 sentence explanation of the problem, (2) a recommended action with justification.

What the LLM output should include

  • Clearly identify whether it's a fill rate, delay, or combined issue
  • Mention the inventory impact (WOS, OOS risk)
  • Reference whether this is a recurring or new issue
  • Choose between Vendor Outreach or Escalate to Merchant
  • Justify the action based on DNP ranking and margin
  • Professional tone, 4 sentences maximum
Important: The COMBINED_ACTION field in the CSV is the ground truth label. Use it to evaluate whether the LLM is making the right decision — do not show it to the LLM as input.
